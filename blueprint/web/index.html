von Neumann Double Commutant Theorem

Jon Bannon Jireh Loreaux

0.1 Introduction

[Blah] The commutant of a set \(S\) of operators is the set of \(T\in B(\mathcal{H})\) such that \(\forall s\in S,\; Ts=sT\).

For \(A\subseteq B(\mathcal{H})\), the weak-operator closure \(\overline{A}^{\mathrm{WOT}}\) is the closure of \(A\) in the weak operator topology on \(B(\mathcal{H})\).

A \(*\)-subalgebra \(A\subseteq B(\mathcal{H})\) is self-adjoint if \(T\in A \Rightarrow T^*\in A\).

0.2 Main Theorem

[von Neumann 1929] The weak operator closure of a self-adjoint \(*\)-subalgebra of \(B(\mathcal{H})\) equals its double commutant.
